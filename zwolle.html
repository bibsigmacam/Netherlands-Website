Zwolle

Vibrant Hanseatic city full of hidden gems

Wander through Zwolle's medieval streets and admire the beautiful architecture. Give your taste buds a treat with local culinary delights and excellent restaurants. Enjoy vibrant festivals and cultural events all year round. Shop at hip boutiques and trendy shops. Zwolle has something for everyone. Visit this multifaceted gem of a city in the green province of Overijssel!

Experience Zwolle like a local

Like everyone from the East Netherlands region, the people of Zwolle are proud of their own language and culture. Down-to-earth Zwolle locals live by the maxim "Just act normal, that's strange enough." In this city, everything revolves around warmth and hospitality. Whatever your reason for visiting, you will immediately feel at home in this warm, hospitable environment.

From Sassenpoort to Peperbus

Take a tour of the city centre and walk past the Sassenpoort, a remnant of Zwolle's ancient city wall. The centuries-old Peperbus tower is part of the Onze Lieve Vrouwe Basilica and, at a height of 75 metres, it's an unmissable landmark. Another real eye-catcher is the beautiful Van der Velde in de Broeren bookshop, located in a monumental church building with a rich history. Other must-sees in Zwolle include the Museum de Fundatie and the ANNO city museum.

As a Hanseatic city, that history is still very much part of Zwolle. The trading centre flourished in the shadow of the imposing Grote Kerk, the church founded in 765 by the missionaries Lebuinus and Marcellinus. With city rights granted in 1230, Zwolle quickly grew into a powerful city, strategically located on a busy trade route. Joining the German Hanseatic League in 1407 ushered in a 'Golden Age'. History and mystery still permeate the city — from medieval buildings to the stories of the Modern Devotion religious movement.

City of students and Blue Fingers

In addition to its rich history, there are some other interesting facts that make Zwolle what it is. For instance, Zwolle is a true student city, where Johan Cele founded the world's first secondary school in the 15th century! Zwolle attracted students from all over the world. People from Zwolle are colloquially known as Blauwvingers (Blue Fingers). According to legend, this name came about when they sold their damaged, old church bells to the people of Kampen. When the people of Zwolle received their payment — a cart full of copper coins — they literally got blue fingers counting them.

Charming walks and vintage shops

Zwolle is known for its many parks and beautiful canals and, thanks to its many green spaces, was named the 'greenest city in Europe' in 2017. Whether you want to get some exercise, go for a picnic, relax or just take a leisurely stroll, the parks in Zwolle offer the perfect setting. Visit Park de Wezenlanden, home to the largest Liberation Festival in the Netherlands, or enjoy the combination of forests and the river IJssel in Park Het Engelse Werk.

Walk and cycle through green spaces

Discover Zwolle the healthy way by walking! Explore the city with routes that take you past forests, along beautiful waterways and through the historic city centre. Follow the 2.4 km Hanseatic city walk and admire the Sassenpoort, canal houses and various monuments. For a more extensive exploration, the 45 km-long Rondje Zwolle takes you through the countryside. You can also follow one of the digital routes to discover charming neighbourhoods and hidden works of art, such as murals and historical sculptures.
