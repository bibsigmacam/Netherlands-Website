Emergency Contacts

The most important number to remember in case of an emergency is 112 (it would be wise to add this to your mobile). This number can be called for free in any kind of emergency — such as when you need the police, fire department or an ambulance. It is comparable to 911 in the USA. They will be able to help you in English as well.

Fun to know: The Netherlands has over 4,000 air raid sirens placed around the country, which can be used in case of a national emergency. Every first Monday of the month these sirens are tested, so don't be startled if you hear them.

Emergency Services & Contact Information

Below are the key emergency and non-emergency contacts you may need during your visit to the Netherlands:

  • Emergency (police, fire, ambulance): 112
  • Non-emergency police: 0900-8844
  • Tourist information (VVV): available in all major cities
  • NS (Dutch Railways) travel information: 0900-9292
  • General information line: 14 + area code (e.g., 14 020 for Amsterdam)

Useful Travel Information

Currency

The Netherlands uses the Euro (€). Card payments — especially contactless — are widely accepted. Many shops and restaurants prefer card over cash. ATMs (pinautomaten) are available in all cities and larger towns.

Language

The official language is Dutch (Nederlands). English is spoken by the vast majority of the population, particularly in cities, tourist areas and by younger generations.

Electricity

The Netherlands uses 230V electricity with Type C and Type F plugs (two round pins). Visitors from outside Europe will need an adapter.

Public Transport

The OV-chipkaart is the universal smart card for trains, trams, metro, and buses throughout the Netherlands. It can be purchased and topped up at all main train stations. The NS app is highly recommended for planning train journeys and buying tickets digitally.

Tipping

Tipping is appreciated but not obligatory. Rounding up the bill or leaving 5–10% for good restaurant service is standard practice. Tips are not generally expected in cafés or bars.
